SAP Data
Data in SAP can be categorized into:

Master data: This data is created and maintained by the end users defining how the business is to be planned and executed. It changes according to business requirements. This includes Material Masters, Resources, Bills of Materials (BOMs), Recipes, Production Versions, and Quotas.

Transactional data: This data results in changes to inventory levels or statuses of inventory. It is the data that is generated as part of daily operations. Examples include production confirmations against process orders, QA approval or rejection of materials, warehouse shipments or receipts of materials.
SIP uses the transactional data to track progress against our schedules.

SIP Views - Planning Data
Data for planning purposes are contained in the MRP views 1 through 4 and the Work Scheduling view.

Data in the four views of MRP are segregated mainly to make it convenient for access - there is no real distinction. Data is grouped together logically in each view. The views contain the following data:

MRP 1 view: contains the general data, material owner, and lot size data
MRP 2 view: gives information about the lead time scheduling and inventory strategy
MRP 3 view: provides information related to planning and availability check of materials
MRP 4 view: provides information for BOM use and material transition
Work Scheduling view: This view is a little different from the MRP views and acts as an enabler of production execution. Hence, this view is more about integration of things and alignment on the execution of how a product is made

Missing Views Report - Error Codes
The Missing Views Report sometimes generates error codes in the column which shows additional information about the view. The screen you see here is the bottom part of the Missing Views Report that you saw on the previous page. This part of the Missing Views Report provides a key to the error codes that are displayed in the body of the report.

These error codes give some indication about why a view is marked as incomplete.

Example: If under B (which is the Accounting View) you see the status as 1(B40), the list of error codes gives information about the meaning of the error code B40. Thus, in this example, the Accounting View is incomplete and the reason is explained by the error code The error code B40 means the price is missing at the costing plant. Someone from Accounting needs to check the MBEW table for standard price in the STFRS field.

Setting Up MRP Group
Each MRP Group is set up in configuration. This means that when a Material Master is created, there will already be set MRP Groups that you can select from.

MRP Groups are included as part of an MRP Profile. MRP Group is not something that you, as a Planner, can dynamically create or change in SAP.

If none of the MRP Groups suit the current business needs, you must inform the Key User. The Key User will submit a ticket and the Service Management Organization will create the required MRP group.

Planning Time Fence
The concept of Planning Time Fence (PTF) was previously reviewed. It provides stability to the planning process for produced materials. SAP dynamically calculates the Planning Time Fence period from
the start date of planning. The "Planning Zone" is the period of time beyond the Planning Time Fence.

Inside the Planning Time Fence ("Firm Zone"), SAP will not generate or change any orders but may provide exception messages to aid in the decision making process.

Therefore, during that period any supply order (planned orders, process orders} are totally under the control of the Planner. SAP cannot make changes to these, but only suggest changes through the use of exception messages.

In the "Planning Zone" planned orders are automatically created or rescheduled by SAP to generate an ideal supply plan.

For sites where production scheduling is done in OMP+ instead of SAP, Planning Time Fence value in MRP1 View would normally cover the whole OMP+ planning horizon. OMP+ has a separate concept of firming production plan for the necessary horizon.

MRP 2 View - Net Requirements Calculation
This discusses safety and requirements in terms of stock and time:

Safety stock: Shows the fixed safety stock quantity
Safety time/act.cov.: Used to maintain inventory days forward coverage
Safety time ind.: Determines what values to include in safety time calculation.
"1" includes only independent requirements (sales forecast and orders only)
"2" includes all requirements (independent and dependent requirements, which include things like inter plant orders or higher level SOM requirements in addition to sales forecast and orders)

The choice of which type to use is aligned with the business and documented in the operating strategy (POSS).

Safety Stock
Safety stock is a part of the inventory strategy. It is a fixed quantity and is excluded from the available quantity (on an MRP List, which you will see later in class)

When the projected inventory is calculated to go below the level of the safety stock, it means it is time to replenish the inventory. Thus, it is like the "low water mark" that says that the inventory needs refilling.

While calculating the level or quantity for safety stock the following elements are considered:

Demand and supply uncertainty
Cycle inventory
Disaster protection

Safety Time
Safety time is a form of safety inventory in terms of days' forward coverage. Unlike safety stock, this is dynamic and depends on the demand. Dynamic means safety inventory quantity varies over time with demand, but protection (days' forward coverage} remains constant.

Following safety time, inventory replenishment is triggered when the number of inventory coverage days is projected to fall below safety time.

It provides an excellent ramp up and ramp down effect to manage material transitions. When phasing out an old material and moving to a new material, safety time is the best safety strategy to use since the amount of safety is driven by the demand. As demand for the old material is reduced and eventually eliminated, the safety will automatically be reduced and ultimately go to zero. As demand for the new material increases, the safety will automatically increase until it reaches the ongoing demand levels.

Other Material Master Views
There are additional Material Master views that are owned by other departments.

These include:

Accounting: This view includes Accounting information like profit center and shows the current inventory valuation for this material.
Purchasing: Basic Purchasing data are displayed in this view.
Sales: General Sales information like Tax data is displayed here.
Warehouse management: This is mainly the storage strategy data.
Costing: The valuation data and standard cost estimate data is present in the Costing view.
QM: The average inspection duration is filled in here.

The Average Inspection Duration (AID) is owned by QA and is stored in the QM view. The GR Processing Time in the MRP 2 view should match the AID in most cases. Prior to production, MRP will define a material as available in inventory based on the GR Processing Time offset. It offsets the finish date to show the available date, using the number of days in the GR Processing Time. When production is confirmed, the date that the material is available in inventory is based on the Average Inspection Duration offset and not the GR Processing Time. If the Average Inspection Duration is different than the GR Processing Time, MRP will have targeted production for the wrong date.
